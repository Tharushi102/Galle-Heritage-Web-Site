Japanese Peace Pagoda

The Unawatuna Japanese Peace Pagoda, also known as the Rumassala Peace Pagoda, is a Buddhist stupa located in Unawatuna, a popular tourist destination in Sri Lanka. The pagoda was constructed to promote peace and harmony among people of different cultures and religions.

The construction of the Japanese Peace Pagoda in Unawatuna began in 2004 and was completed in 2005. It was built by the Nipponzan Myohoji Buddhist Order, a Japanese sect of Buddhism known for their promotion of peace and non-violence. The pagoda is one of the many peace pagodas built around the world by this order.

The Unawatuna Peace Pagoda stands on a hilltop overlooking the ocean and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. It is a popular attraction for tourists and visitors who come to admire its architectural beauty and peaceful ambiance. The pagoda is painted in white, which symbolizes purity and peace.

Inside the pagoda, there is a large statue of the Buddha and several smaller statues depicting important events from his life. The walls of the pagoda are adorned with colorful murals depicting Buddhist teachings and stories. Visitors can explore the pagoda and take part in meditation and prayers if they wish.

The Unawatuna Japanese Peace Pagoda serves as a symbol of peace and unity in Sri Lanka and attracts both Buddhists and non-Buddhists who seek solace and tranquility. It is not only a religious site but also a cultural landmark that promotes understanding and tolerance among different communities.
