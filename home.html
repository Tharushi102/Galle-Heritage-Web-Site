Galle City

Galle, a compelling tourist destination in Sri Lanka, is located on the southwest coast of the island, 119 km south of Colombo. It is blessed with a range of attractions, including beautiful beaches, breathtaking coral reefs, virgin jungles, and the huge citadel. Galle, Sri Lanka's fourth-largest city after the capital Colombo, Kandy, and Jaffna, serves as the district capital of Galle District and the administrative center of Southern Province. The two most visited tourist destinations in the area are Hikkaduwa and Unawatuna.

At a glance

  • - Area : 16.52 km2
  • - Population : Approx 100,000
  • - Distance : 100 km south of Colombo (1-hr drive on Highway)
  • - Province : Southern Province
  • - Climate : Hot and humid over the year reaching max temperature up to 31oC and rainy from May to September.

Galle's history extends back to the 14th century; at that time, it was Tarshish's historic seaport, and King Solomon used it to bring in valuables including elephants, peacocks, and jewels. Galle harbor was utilized by Persians, Arabs, Greeks, Romans, Malays, Indians, and Chinese for trade during the fourteenth century. Early in the year 1400 BC, the seaport of Galle was crucial in the exportation of spices, particularly cinnamon, from Sri Lanka. Galle's modern history, however, began in the 16th century. With the arrival of Lourenco de Almeida in 1505, Portuguese navigators fortified Galle in order to protect the northern landside of the peninsula. However, when the Dutch came in Galle in 1640, they overthrew them. Before the British arrived, the 18th century was when Galle reached the height of its development. Galle, which was named a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the "cultural category" in 1988 and is most known for its centuries-old fort, is now a beautiful city that draws a large number of both domestic and international visitors. South Asian cultures and European architectural styles come together beautifully in Galle. While on vacation in this fortified city of Galle, there are many things to see and do.


Getting to Galle

You can use a bus, train, or a seaplane to get to Galle. The Colombo Central Bus Station (private and public), located in Pettah, is the departure point for buses traveling on route No. 2 to Galle. From Kaduwela and Maharagama, luxurious buses go through the Southern Expressway in just one hour. Trains heading to Galle depart from the Fort Railway Station and go there in 2 12 hours (inter-city express). Another option is to take a seaplane from Peliyagoda, which takes off for Galle from the Kelani River. Bookings are required for seaplanes.


Getting Around in Galle

The major bus terminal for Galle is in the city center, and from there, buses (both regular and air-conditioned intercity buses) depart for surrounding regions, Galle's suburbs, and the island's largest towns, including Colombo, Kandy, Anuradhpura, Trincomalee, etc. The main bus terminal offers luxury buses to Kaduwela and Maharagama that travel to Colombo via the Southern Expressway. Additionally, taxis are available for hiring, and the city's surroundings are filled with three-wheelers (Tuk Tuks). Short-distance trips are best served by tuk tuks with a three-person capacity. There are also self-driving automobiles.
